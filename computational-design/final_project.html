Introduction

As an avant-garde and progressive approaches in architectural design, application of minimal surfaces and computational design have brought professionals from a vast number of fields together in the objective of designing and construction of buildings. Subsequently, the application of such surfaces in design requires architects attention in mastering parametric geometries in architecture and not just the classical solids.

The fulfillment of this project requires your completion of all works on this course in general and specifically ‘1.4 subchapter and related exercises ’. You can find the pre-research done on the topic (for students that did not manage to work on the task) at this link: 1.5: Exercise on Contemporary and Parametric Geometries in Architecture. Group Assignment

The project is a group assignment and groups are already formed. The list of groups and members can be found from this link: Groups and members.

Each group will build on the research that was started previously on Contemporary and Parametric Geometries in Architecture: Minimal surfaces (zero mean surfaces). The eleven Minimal surfaces (zero mean surfaces) that were studied during the exercise were: CATENOIDS, HELICOIDS, TRIPLY PERIODIC SURFACES, RIEMANN’S SURFACE, ENNEPER SURFACE, HENNEBERG SURFACE, BOUR’S SURFACE, GYROID, SADDLE TOWERs, COSTA’S SURFACE, CHEN GACKSTATTER SURFACES.
Each group shall be assigned to one of these surfaces without any repetition. In such a way that all of the above mentioned mean zero surfaces are covered.

The scope of the work includes:

Part one (This part is for all class members)

1. Further research on the assigned surface,
1.1. Definition, property, application, etc.
2. Digital model of the surface, and
2.1. Parametric Digital model of the surface should be prepared with required illustrations.
3. Physical construction/ model of the surface:
3.1. Here the effective method of transformation of the digital model to the real model will be considered.
3.2. Digital fabrication tools (laser cutter, 3D printer, CNC milling, etc) are preferable. However, the scale at which the prototype is going to be manufactured will require long time and large amount of material making the cost of the method expensive. Therefore, it is advised that students look in to the option of transferring the digital information by making use of locally available materials and manual methods.

Part two (This is for students that failed to work on their mini-research for 1.4 subchapter and related exercises’)

To make up for the exercise you failed to work on, you are expected to model and construct a product of your liking (Architectural component, furniture, fixture, and etc.) as per discussion held in class.
