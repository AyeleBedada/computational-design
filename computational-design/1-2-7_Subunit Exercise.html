1.2.7, Exercise on parametric modeling/ design- Revision on the interpretation of the chess piece/ set



On the 1.3 subchapters of this course, you worked on your personal interpretation of the chess set/ pieces and the re-imagining/ recreation of the Chris Lasch's table. After you have gone through the recent subunits of the course, it is expected that you can better apply computational design principles in to the task. As such for this task, you will revise your work 'the re-imagining of the chess piece' and submit your work through the provided channel below.


Please be reminded that:
(1) If you are using revit for this task, do not limit yourself by making the choice of template to be only the metric furniture. You can have more freedom if you use the in-place mass/ conceptual mass/ metric (generic) adaptive templates.
(2) Your consideration for the scale of the project should not just be the chess set as furniture, you can consider the pieces as large as landscape elements and other large size objects or even building blocks.



Consider the following points stated by Chris Lasch as part of your lecture in Sub-Chapter 1.6, Pioneers of Computational Design in architecture of this course to shape your work process.


• Story telling through computational architecture (projects).
• Relationship between architecture, scripting, algorism, recursive geometry and the like.
• Scripting can be both visual and non-visual.
• Strong formal language makes the design anti-social and anti-collaborative.
• Components are various like snowflakes but similar Computational design principle breaks down elements to grains and leaves chance for new opportunity of design and shapes Interpolation of parts to the whole.
• Computational design did to the physical world what time did to the ruins.
• Opportunity to design free with new principles.
• Reinvention in materials and how they got energy is transforming our culture. [Stone age, Bronze age, the industrial age, Silicon age, Nano age (the next age)-Age of molecular control]
• Materials are not sculpted but grown.
• Bottom up rule replaces the top down rule.
• Parts come together in rhythm to make a better whole.
• No hierarchy of building elements but holistic forms.
• If this is the age of makers we like the idea of being ruiners.
• We atomize the concept of a project in order to build them up in to something we would not have been able to build before. We do this to disrupt our idea of what that thing should have been in the first place.
• This idea for an architecture that needs to be reformed after falling apart is what we are about and what we continue to investigate.
