1.3: All exercises for parametric modeling without scripting

On your lecture notes "working with parametric families without scripting or programming ( visual or hard coding)", six specific exercises are indicated. This page is prepared as an extraction/ summery and submission instruction of those exercises.

All parametric models you will prepare in the following exercises need to demonstrate: size, material and visibility parameters.Furthermore you have to demonstrate your modeling skill in: Extrusion, blend, revolve, sweep, swept blend to both solids and voids.

(A)Six face Cube with particle (Cubic mass and six particles):
Six parametric families for six faces of a cubic mass. For this exercise, you are expected to prepare/ design six original parametric families and apply them on a cubic mass one per each faces of the cubic mass as demonstrated in class.
(B) Furniture/fixture family (Three furniture/ fixture family models separately and loaded in to a project(for image)):
You can model the three furniture in separate furniture template and load them on to one project. For this exercise, you are expected to prepare/ design three furniture families by taking measured data from your favorite furniture.
(C) Two point reactor system family (Two point reactor family applied on a building facade as demonstrated in class):
Your parametric models needs to demonstrate: size, material and visibility parameters. For this exercise, you are expected to prepare/ design a two point reactor system family and apply it to a building facade.
(D) Nested family (Nested family applied on a building facade as demonstrated in class):
For this exercise, you are expected to prepare/ design a nested family and apply it on a building facade.
(E) Table by Chris Lasch (Refer the image of the table under consideration on your lecture note):
The table shall not be similar to Chris Lasch's table by size. It should be similar to Chris Lasch's table in concept and technique.
(F) Interpreted Chess set:
For this exercise, you are expected to prepare/ design a chess set by interpreting the traditional chess set according to your interest in showing your mass modeling skills/ design in general.
