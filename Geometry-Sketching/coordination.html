2.7.1, Coordination of Architectural, Mechanical, electrical and plumbing (MEP) and road documents.

In this subunit, you will discuss about how different drawings and documents from different disciplines of the built environment such as: Architectural, electrical, sanitary and road are put together for coordination and discussion among the representatives of the respective disciplines.

Discuss the following topics at (1) Site (urban, neighborhood) level and (2) Building level:

(1) Discussion at site (urban, neighborhood) level


(a) Relationship between site topography, sanitary water storage and supply (municipality, water reservoir, etc.) and distribution, waste water deposal/ treatment. All conditions of these relationships should be answered with proper specification of material and design approaches. The drawing should indicate these solutions in detail through text, line, symbols and hatches as indicated in the slides below.
(b) Relationship between road layout (primary and secondary), topography and building floor finish level and natural ground level. Check for seamless access between road levels, landscape areas, buildings, verandahs and the like. All conditions of these relationships should be answered with proper specification of material and design approaches. The drawing should indicate these solutions in detail through text, line, symbols and hatches.
(c) Relationship between roads, topography surface run off, ditches, culverts, etc.
(d) Relationship between number of blocks (as clustered by the urban designer or architect) with sub electric power distribution boards and common electric distribution boards.
(e) Relationship between roads and street lights and the like.

(2) Discussion at building level


(a) Relationship between fresh water supply lines, futures and fittings with architectural (Building) compacts and electrical components. The drawing should indicate these solutions in detail through text, line, symbols and hatches as indicated in the slides below.
(b) Relationship between Electric power components such as sockets, lights, switches, bells, internet access points and the like with architectural/ building components and spaces. The drawing should indicate these solutions in detail through text, line, symbols and hatches as indicated in the slides below.
