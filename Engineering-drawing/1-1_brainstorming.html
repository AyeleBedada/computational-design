1.1: Brainstorming session on parameters and constraints

At the end of your first class on Computational design in architecture, publish a small task of your own on one of the the padlets at the end of this page. Your work should fit in to the scope of the discussions in class.

Summary of the brainstorming session:

On this brainstorming session on fundamentals of parameters and constraints, you discussed about: Geometric Constraints, Dimensional Constraints, and Expressions.

Geometric constraints allow you to fix elements of your design as horizontal, vertical, parallel, perpendicular or at angle to a given reference. Furthermore they allow points or points and other objects to coincide with each other. Other geometric constraints include: collinear, concentric, symmetrical, equal, tangent and smooth relationship between objects of your design.

Dimensional constraints allow you to declare variables that represent the objects in your design and assign dimensional values to them. These can be linear dimensions, diameters / radius, angles, and other parameters.

By making use of expressions (formulas) that use the declared variables mentioned above and the values assigned to them, you can create a parametric relationship between objects of your design allowing you to come up with different options and scenarios. It is also important to note the advantage the geometrical constraints you placed offer in this activity.

Assignment to be carried out by students:

For this week's activity, you should understand the basics/ fundamentals of parameters and create a pattern by employing geometric, dimensional constraints and expressions.
